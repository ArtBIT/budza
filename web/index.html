Install firmware on your Budz

  1. 1
    Plug in your badge

    Connect it to your computer with a USB-C cable.

  2. 2
    Enter flash mode

    Hold BOOT, tap RESET, release BOOT. The badge screen will go dark — that's normal.

  3. 3
    Click the button below

    A port picker will appear. Select the one that looks like your badge:

    • Windows COM3, COM4, … — any new COM port that appeared after plugging in
    • Linux /dev/ttyACM0 or /dev/ttyUSB0
    • Either USB JTAG/serial debug unit or USB Serial Device

    Flashing takes ~30 s. The badge reboots automatically when done.

Requires Chrome or Edge (WebSerial API).

Building the firmware yourself

In Arduino IDE open badge/badge.ino, then choose Sketch → Export Compiled Binary (Alt+Ctrl+S). Then from the repo root run: 

make firmware

That copies the three required files into web/firmware/. Alternatively, copy them manually:

  • badge.ino.bootloader.bin → firmware/bootloader.bin
  • badge.ino.partitions.bin → firmware/partitions.bin
  • badge.ino.bin → firmware/firmware.bin

Add photos & videos to your Budz

Make sure your badge's WiFi is on: swipe up on the badge → tap WiFi ON. Then connect your computer to the Budz-XXXX network and click Upload below.
